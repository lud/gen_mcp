# `subscriptions/listen`: the long-lived SSE stream

> **STATUS: DRAFT — NOT READY FOR IMPLEMENTATION.** Deferred out of spec 003 so
> the transport rework stays focused on stateless request/response. Refine before
> implementing. This is the **only surviving server-push path** after the
> standalone GET SSE stream was removed in 2026-07-28.

## What it is

Spec source: `docs/specification/draft/basic/transports.mdx`
(rendered: <https://modelcontextprotocol.io/specification/draft/basic/transports>).

The 2026-07-28 revision **removed the standalone GET SSE stream**. Long-lived
server→client notifications now require an explicit opt-in request:

> "Long-lived notification streams are obtained by sending a `subscriptions/listen`
> request. The server's response is itself an SSE stream that stays open and
> delivers the change notifications the client opted in to (such as
> `notifications/tools/list_changed` or `notifications/resources/updated`)."

Contrast with **request-scoped** notifications (`notifications/progress`,
`notifications/message`): those flow **only** on the response stream of the
request they relate to and are handled in spec 003 — they are **not** delivered on
the listen stream.

## Why it is its own spec

- It is a **genuinely long-lived connection**, unlike the per-request SSE in 003
  (which lives for the duration of one request). Different lifecycle, keepalive,
  and teardown concerns.
- Without it, list-change / resource-update notifications simply do not reach
  clients. That is an acceptable temporary gap while 003/004/005 land — the
  stateless core works without it — but it must be tracked.

## Sketch (to refine)

- New `subscriptions/listen` request entity (verify it exists in the spec 002
  generated vocabulary; generate if missing).
- The transport opens an SSE stream for the request and keeps it open (reuse the
  `stream_loop` / keepalive machinery retained in spec 003).
- A **subscription filter**: the client opts into specific notification types;
  the server must only push those. Where is the filter stored? In a stateless
  world the subscription itself is connection-scoped state held by the
  request-handling process for the life of the stream — acceptable because it
  dies with the connection (no cross-request/session state).
- **Fan-out source (OPEN):** what produces `tools/list_changed` /
  `resources/updated` in `GenMCP.Suite`? Today list changes are driven by the
  (being-deleted) session machinery. Need a stateless pub/sub: where do repos
  announce changes, and how does an open listen stream subscribe to that without
  reintroducing a per-session registry? (Phoenix.PubSub-style topic? A
  `:pg`/`Registry` of open listen streams?)

## Open questions (resolve before implementation)

1. Does reintroducing a process/registry for open listen streams violate the
   "no GenServer / no registry" goal of the fork? (It is connection-scoped, not
   session-scoped — likely acceptable, but state the boundary explicitly.)
2. What is the notification-source design in `Suite` once `SessionController` and
   `Mux.Session` are gone?
3. Keepalive / idle-timeout / max-duration policy for a long-lived stream.
4. Cancellation semantics: closing the stream cancels the subscription
   (consistent with 003's per-request "close = cancel").
5. Auth/scoping: which clients may subscribe to what.

## Acceptance criteria (provisional)

- A client can `subscriptions/listen` with a filter and receive only the
  opted-in change notifications on a long-lived SSE stream.
- Closing the stream cleanly tears down the subscription with no leaked process.
- No per-session registry is reintroduced (connection-scoped state only).
